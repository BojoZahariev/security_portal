Children visiting the workplace - Declaration

All colleagues bringing child to work must sign the statement below to confirm that they have read and understood their responsibilities as outlined in the Children Visiting the Workplace Policy.

Key Points

  1. Approval has been obtained prior to this visit by the relevant manager.
  2. You will be responsible for the child at all times and must not leave your child alone or supervised by another colleague. If you are required to attend a meeting during your visit, alternative childcare arrangements outside of the workplace should be made.
  3. You will be briefed on the fire and evacuation procedure and assembly points and must take responsibility for evacuating the child during any emergency.Security will be responsible for briefing you of the relevant health and safety procedures.
  4. In the event of an evacuation, caution should be given when making an exit from the building.
  5. Caution should be given when using pushchairs to avid obstructions.
  6. Should a child be injured whilst on Asda's premises, this must be reported to reception to be logged who will escalate to City help desk.
  7. Children must be supervised at all times in hazardous areas. These for example include:
    1. Escalators
    2. Revolving doors
    3. Stair wells
    4. Landings

    Extra caution should be taken and children must not be left alone or able to walk/run freely without supervision

    Children would not be allowed to enter any areas where hazardous substances are being used or if their presence could cause harm to the child. For example:

    1. Test kitchen
    2. Car park - unsupervised
    3. Escalators - when using pushchairs
  8. Children must not be allowed to touch any work-related equipment, articles or waste.
  9. Children must not cause disruption of the work area or the work of other colleagues.
  10. The relevant manager/security can request you to remove the child immediately from the work area if any of the above conditions are being breached.

I confirm that i have read and understood the points outlined above and the Children visiting the Workplace policy. I accept that I will be responsible at all times for the child I am supervising.
